Bhaktapur

Nepal's Living Heritage City

The Medieval Jewel of Nepal

Bhaktapur, located in the Kathmandu Valley, is one of Nepal's best preserved medieval cities and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This ancient Newari kingdom boasts magnificent temples, palaces and artistic courtyards dating back to the 12th century.

The city remains a living museum of traditional Newari culture, with artisans continuing centuries-old crafts like pottery and wood carving.

"a glimpse of what a royal Nepalese city looked like 800 years ago"

A Living History

Founded in the 12th century, Bhaktapur was the capital of Nepal during the great Malla Kingdom until the 15th century. The city's golden age saw the construction of its iconic temples, palaces and public squares.

Unlike Kathmandu and Patan, Bhaktapur avoided rapid modernization and retains its traditional urban fabric with brick-paved streets, historic water systems, and vernacular Newari architecture.

Visitor Information

Best Time to Visit

• September-November (pleasant weather)

• February-April (spring festival season)

• Bisket Jatra (April) showcases spectacular chariot festival

Estimated Costs

• Entrance fee: ~$15 USD for foreigners

• Guided tours: ~$25/day

• Heritage hotels: $50-150/night

• Local eateries: $3-8 per meal

Travel Tips

• Stay overnight to see daily life unfold

• Visit Pottery Square in the morning

• Explore Taumadhi Square at dusk

• Try Juju Dhau (royal curd) - Bhaktapur's specialty

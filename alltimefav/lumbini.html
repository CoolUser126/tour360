Lumbini

The Sacred Birthplace of Buddha

The Spiritual Heart of Buddhism

Lumbini, located in the Terai plains of southern Nepal, is one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites for Buddhists worldwide. It was here that Queen Maya Devi gave birth to Siddhartha Gautama in 623 BCE, who would later become the Buddha.

This UNESCO World Heritage Site encompasses monasteries, archaeological remains, and sacred ponds that create a serene atmosphere of peace and meditation.

"Lumbini stands as a testimony to Buddha's message of peace and compassion"

Historical Significance

The sacred site was rediscovered in 1896 when archaeologists identified the Ashoka Pillar erected by Emperor Ashoka in 249 BCE to commemorate his pilgrimage to Buddha's birthplace.

Excavations revealed the Maya Devi Temple built directly over the spot where Buddha was born, with the Marker Stone and Nativity Sculpture marking the exact location.

Visitor Information

Best Time to Visit

• October to March (pleasant weather)

• Avoid monsoon season (June-September)

• Buddha Purnima festival (April/May) is extra special

Estimated Costs

• Entrance fee: ~$10 USD

• Guides: ~$20/day

• Basic hotel: $30-50/night

• Meals: $5-10 per person

Travel Tips

• Stay 2-3 days for full experience

• Dress modestly (shoulders/knees covered)

• Remove shoes at sacred sites

• Morning visits recommended for peaceful atmosphere

Kathmandu

Where Temples Touch the Sky

A Living Museum of Newari Heritage

Kathmandu, the capital of Nepal, is a mesmerizing blend of ancient traditions and modern energy. This UNESCO-listed valley city enchants visitors with its exquisite pagoda temples, bustling bazaars, and rich cultural tapestry woven over centuries.

The air thrums with temple bells, incense and the prayers of pilgrims at sacred sites like Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple) and Pashupatinath. Every corner reveals artistic marvels - intricate wood carvings, gilded statues, and timeless courtyard palaces.

"Lumbini stands as a testimony to Buddha's message of peace and compassion"

Timeless City of Temples

Founded over 2000 years ago, Kathmandu flourished as the crossroads of Himalayan trade routes. The medieval Malla kings built magnificent Durbar Squares adorned with towering temples showcasing Newari architectural mastery.

The city preserves vibrant traditions - Kumari worship with living goddesses, sacred masked dances of Indra Jatra festival, and intricate crafting of metal statues and thangka paintings passed down through generations of Newari artisans.

Visitor Information

Best Time to Visit

• September-November (pleasant weather)

• February-April (spring festival season)

• Bisket Jatra (April) showcases spectacular chariot festival

Estimated Costs

• Entrance fee: ~$15 USD for foreigners

• Guided tours: ~$25/day

• Heritage hotels: $50-150/night

• Local eateries: $3-8 per meal

Travel Tips

• Stay overnight to see daily life unfold

• Visit Pottery Square in the morning

• Explore Taumadhi Square at dusk

• Try Juju Dhau (royal curd) - Bhaktapur's specialty

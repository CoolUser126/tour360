Chitwan

Nepal's Wildlife Paradise

Jungle Safari Wonderland

Chitwan National Park, Nepal's first national park established in 1973, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its incredible biodiversity. The park is home to rare animals like the one-horned rhinoceros, Bengal tigers, and over 500 bird species.

Visitors can experience thrilling jungle safaris, elephant rides, canoe trips, and traditional Tharu cultural performances in this biodiverse Terai region.

Tharu Culture

The indigenous Tharu people have inhabited the Chitwan region for centuries, developing unique cultural traditions and sustainable practices for living alongside wildlife. Their stilt-house villages and colorful festivals showcase a vibrant heritage.

The Tharu Museum offers insight into their traditional lifestyles including fishing techniques, farming methods, and beautiful handicrafts made from jungle materials.

Visitor Information

Best Time to Visit

• September-November (pleasant weather)

• February-April (spring festival season)

• Bisket Jatra (April) showcases spectacular chariot festival

Estimated Costs

• Entrance fee: ~$15 USD for foreigners

• Guided tours: ~$25/day

• Heritage hotels: $50-150/night

• Local eateries: $3-8 per meal

Travel Tips

• Stay overnight to see daily life unfold

• Visit Pottery Square in the morning

• Explore Taumadhi Square at dusk

• Try Juju Dhau (royal curd) - Bhaktapur's specialty

Everest Region

The Roof of the World

The World's Highest Adventure

The Everest region, home to the world's highest peak at 8,848m, is Nepal's most spectacular trekking destination. This UNESCO-listed Sagarmatha National Park offers breathtaking landscapes, Sherpa culture, and challenging high-altitude adventures.

The region provides various trek routes from moderate trails to the extreme Everest Base Camp trek, passing through picturesque villages, suspension bridges, and Buddhist monasteries.

"Lumbini stands as a testimony to Buddha's message of peace and compassion"

Sherpa Homeland

The Everest region has been home to Sherpa communities for centuries, who migrated from Tibet carrying with them rich Buddhist traditions. Their mountain skills and acclimatization have made them legendary Himalayan guides.

The first successful ascent by Hillary and Tenzing in 1953 put Everest on the world map, and the region has since become a mecca for mountaineers while maintaining its spiritual character.

Visitor Information

Best Time to Visit

• September-November (pleasant weather)

• February-April (spring festival season)

• Bisket Jatra (April) showcases spectacular chariot festival

Estimated Costs

• Entrance fee: ~$15 USD for foreigners

• Guided tours: ~$25/day

• Heritage hotels: $50-150/night

• Local eateries: $3-8 per meal

Travel Tips

• Stay overnight to see daily life unfold

• Visit Pottery Square in the morning

• Explore Taumadhi Square at dusk

• Try Juju Dhau (royal curd) - Bhaktapur's specialty

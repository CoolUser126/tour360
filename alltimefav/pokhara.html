Pokhara

Nepal's Lakeside Paradise

Gateway to the Himalayas

Pokhara, Nepal's second largest city, is renowned for its spectacular natural beauty with the Annapurna mountain range as its backdrop and the serene Phewa Lake at its center. This adventure hub offers everything from peaceful lakeside relaxation to thrilling outdoor sports.

The city serves as the gateway to the Annapurna Circuit trek while offering its own attractions including caves, waterfalls, and breathtaking viewpoints that showcase Himalayan panoramas.

"On Earth there is no heaven but there are pieces of it"

Cultural Melting Pot

Pokhara has long been a cultural crossroads, home to diverse ethnic groups including Gurungs, Magars, and Newars, each contributing to the city's rich traditions. The city evolved from a trading post on the India-Tibet route to Nepal's premier tourist destination.

The fascinating International Mountain Museum chronicles Pokhara's mountaineering history while the Gurkha Memorial Museum honors Nepal's legendary soldiers.

Visitor Information

Best Time to Visit

• September-November (pleasant weather)

• February-April (spring festival season)

• Avoid monsoon (June-August) due to heavy rains

Estimated Costs

• Entrance fee: ~$15 USD for foreigners

• Trekking permits: $20-50 USD depending on route

• Accommodation: $10-50 per night

• Local eateries: $3-8 per meal

Travel Tips

• Stay overnight to see daily life unfold

• Visit Pottery Square in the morning

• Explore Bhaktapur Durbar Square at sunrise

• Dress modestly when visiting temples

• Try local delicacies like Yomari and King Curd
